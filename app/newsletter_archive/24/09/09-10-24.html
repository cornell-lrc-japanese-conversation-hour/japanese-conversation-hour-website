四字熟語
よじじゅくご

四字熟語 are four-character compounds or idiomatic phrases in Japanese, made up of kanji. Similar expressions exist in other East Asian languages, like Chinese Chengyu (成语, chéngyǔ) and Korean Sajaseong-eo (사자성어, 四字成語).

Learning 四字熟語 is a fun way to explore kanji’s meanings and pronunciation, and an opportunity to sound more sophisticated by using them!😎 Many 四字熟語 have interesting backstories, offering a glimpse into Japanese culture!😊

This week, 和会話教室 would like to introduce four common 四字熟語 featuring animal-related kanji.

🐦 一石二鳥
いっせきにちょう

Literal & idiomatic meaning: to kill two(二) birds(鳥) with one(一) stone(石); to solve two tasks with a single action.

Another 四字熟語 with the same meaning is 一挙両得(いっきょりょうとく).

🐎 馬耳東風
ばじとうふう

Literal meaning: spring wind(= east wind, 東風) blowing in horse(馬)’s ears(耳).
Idiomatic meaning: utter indifference; talking to a wall; ignore others’ words.

🐁 窮鼠噛猫
きゅうそねこをかむ

Literal & idiomatic meaning: a cornered rat will bite the cat; one who is cornered will fight like a devil.

This 四字熟語 can be written as the phrase “窮鼠猫を噛む,” read in the same way with the same meaning.

🐯 虎視眈眈 or 虎視眈々
こしたんたん

Literal meaning: tiger(虎) watching intently(視, 眈々)

Idiomatic meaning: carefully watch for an opportunity; covetously wait and watch (until the enemy shows a weakness)
