11/3 文化の日 (ぶんかのひ; bunkanohi)
Bora Shin

文化の日, or Culture Day, is a national holiday in Japan that was established in 1948 for cherishing culture, arts, and academic achievements. The origins of 文化の日 trace back to Emperor Meiji’s birthday, which was also celebrated on November 3rd.

On 文化の日, award ceremonies are held at 皇居 (こうきょ; koukyo), the Imperial Palace in Tokyo. The government presents 文化勲章 (ぶんかくんしょう; bunga kunsyou), Order of Culture, to individuals who have made significant contributions to cultural development and intellectual growth. The awards are often granted by the emperor himself, and recognize achievements in diverse areas such as science, arts, literature, and technology.

Various events, such as festivals and exhibitions, are centered around this day as well. For instance, 文化庁芸術祭 (ぶんかちょうげいじゅつさい; bunkachou geijyutsisai), Japan Media Arts Festival, is hosted by 文化庁 (ぶんかちょう; bunkachou), Agency for Cultural Affairs, every year. Established in 1946, 文化庁芸術祭 aims to showcase and preserve performance arts, theater, music, dance, and films. As part of the event, the visitors can take a look at award ceremonies, screenings, workshops, and lectures.

Besides 文化庁芸術祭, many museums and cultural institutions offer free admission on 文化の日, and hold entertaining programs, performances, and festivals showcasing both traditional arts and modern installations.
