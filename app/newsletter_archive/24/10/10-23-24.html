Who's Who in the Contemporary Japanese Fiction Scene
Tami Takada

Murakami Haruki isn’t the only Japanese fiction writer active today. Here, we’ll introduce three authors of contemporary fiction popular in Japan today!

池井戸潤（いけいどじゅん） | Ikeido Jun

Former banker Ikeido Jun debuted in 1998 with 「果つる底なき」（はつるそこなき）, a murder mystery set in the finance world. Since then, Ikeido has published multiple series and standalone works often spotlighting businessmen. His well-loved 下町（したまち）ロケット series following the journey of a mid-sized startup garnered critical acclaim with the prestigious 直木賞 (Naoki Prize) in 2010. Many of his works, including 「下町ロケット」, 「アキラとあきら」, and 「鉄の骨」, have also been adapted into TV shows.

辻村深月（つじむらみづき） | Tsujimura Mizuki

Tsujimura Mizuki is a prolific writer known for her sometimes fantastical stories that nevertheless acutely depict the emotional states of her characters. After just missing out on the 直木賞 in 2009 with a mystery novel following the strained friendship between two rural women (「ゼロ、ハチ、ゼロ、ナナ。」), she won the prize in 2012 with her short story collection depicting how five women were pushed to crime in「鍵（かぎ）のない夢を見る」. More recently, her fantastical novel 「かがみの孤城」centering on seven students who avoid school (不登校) was adapted into an animated movie, Lonely Castle in the Mirror (2022).

小川洋子（おがわようこ） | Ogawa Yoko

While Tsujimura and Ikeido tend to set their novels in modern-day Japanese society, Ogawa Yoko often chooses looser settings that allow her to explore sometimes terrifying implications. Some of her major works include 「密（ひそ）やかな結晶（けっしょう）」(translated as The Memory Police in English), which considers what remains under the surveillance and pressure of a violent political regime, as well as 「博士の愛した数織」 (translated as The Housekeeper and the Professor), a short novel depicting the bond between a mathematician with a peculiar brand of dementia and his housekeeper’s family.
