One-Word Abbreviations 🤓📱 ~ マ？り！一文字の略語とかなんちゃら ~
Sara Goto

Abbreviations in the Japanese language are very common, longer vocabulary usually being shortened to a few characters for easier written or oral communication. Some abbreviated forms are used in general contexts, such as:



高校　＝　高等学校（こうとうがっこう） → high school
エアコン　＝　エアーコンディショナー → air conditioner
チューハイ　＝　焼酎（しょうちゅう）ハイボール → shochu highball

(Many of these abbreviations are more sound-focused and less meaning-focused, taking parts that are easier to voice out loud instead of retaining the parts of vocabulary that describe the overall meaning!)



However, other abbreviations are much more informal and considered to be slang for certain subcultures (younger people, internet, etc.) that may even be harder to interpret, such as:



ダントツ　＝　断然（だんぜん）トップ → absolute top
カワボ　＝　かわいいボイス → cute voice
そっ閉じ　＝　そっと閉じる → softly close

The following are the latter type, subculture-specific one-word-abbreviations used in informal communication. Try to figure out patterns for taking the first character or the last character in these abbreviations! Are they easier to pronounce? Are certain characters more common than others? Answers are attached at the end.



_？＝　それで？ → and?
_　＝　それな → real
_　＝　そうだね → so true
_？＝　マジで？ → really?
_　＝　了解（りょうかい） → understood
_　＝　うん → ok

They’ll likely be understood in casual conversation for younger people, so feel free to utilize them!! (JPCH will not ensure successful results for everyone)
