[kiɾäkiɾäne̞ːmɯ̟]—an unconventional first name, usually using nontraditional kanji to fit the sounds of Western names, random words, or pop culture references. Can also refer to a name with a blatantly negative connotation (such as “Akuma”).

EXAMPLES
「キラキラネームって読みづらいから苦労するよね」
Unusual Japanese names are so hard to read from the kanji…
「うちの子［詩］って書いて［ぽえむ］って読むんだけど、キラキラネームかな？」
Is this a weird baby name—reading the kanji ‘shi’ for poem, literally as ‘Poemu’?

解答 vs. 回答

Bora Shin
“解答” and “回答” are both read as “かいとう[kai-tou],” and includes the same character “答,” meaning “answer.” Yet, they slightly differ in meaning. “解答” means the right answer to the question (問いの答え). For instance, we may use the term “解答” to refer to the correct answer for an exam question. On the other hand, “回答” refers to the reply (返事) (to a question). The answer one can get from a survey (アンケート) or an informative inquiry (問い合わせ) would be “回答.”

EXAMPLES
「その問題の正しい解答を教えてください」
Please tell me the correct answer for that question
「クラスの皆さんから回答をもらいました」
I got a reply from everyone in my class
