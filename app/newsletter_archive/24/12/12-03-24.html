日本の方言
Bora Shin

方言 (hōgen) refers to regional dialects of the Japanese language. Depending on the geographical area, 方言 varies in pronunciation, vocabulary, grammar, and sometimes intonation. These dialects developed because people in different regions of Japan used to have limited interaction due to the country's mountainous terrain and island geography.

標準語 (hyōjungo) is the standard Japanese and is on the Tokyo dialect. 標準語 is used in formal settings and is taught in schools.

Unlike 標準語, 方言 is sometimes stigmatized or seen as informal and non-professional; however, it is increasingly appreciated as a cultural treasure that reflects regional identity.

Japan is divided into 都道府県 (todōfuken), or 47 prefectures, each one with unique cultural, geographical, and historical characteristics. In this advertisement, people from different 都道府県 say「お家WIFIってとっても早いんだよ。快適だから繋げてね。」(Home Wi-Fi is super fast. It's comfortable, so go ahead and connect to it!) in 47 different dialects.
