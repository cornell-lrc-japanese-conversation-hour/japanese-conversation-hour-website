BREAKING NEWS

Tami Takada
In place of a yearbook, many schools in Japan compile a graduation anthology (卒業文集) containing writings from each student about where they see themselves in the future, what they enjoyed during their time at school, and more. Recently, however, the anthology has come under fire for overstressing teachers. Toyosaki East Elementary School in Osaka has gone as far as to ban the creation of the anthology, leading other schools to question if they should follow suit.
