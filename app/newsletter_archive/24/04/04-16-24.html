Tami Takada
ダウンロード
Hashiba Hideyoshi—perhaps better known by his title Toyotomi Hideyoshi—was originally a servant of Oda Nobunaga during the warring-states period. Following Nobunaga’s assassination in 1582 (本能寺の変), Hideyoshi took over the unification of Japan (天下統一) and even led an attempt to invade China through Korea (文禄・慶長の役). Just before his passing in 1598, Hideyoshi held a flower viewing event at Kyoto’s Daigo temple (醍醐の花見), which is depicted above.

Source: Haruo Sasayama, et al., Shōsetsu Nihonshi Kaiteiban (Tokyo: Yamakawa Shuppansha, 2022), 160-5.

Bora Shin
"あり得る" means there is a certain possibility of something happening(その可能性がある). But how would you read "あり得る" in Japanese? It is often read as "ありえる[arieru]"; yet, the correct reading sounds "ありうる[ariuru]." Although it is more appropriate to read "ありうる," it is often said "ありえる" in spoken language, or even in a shortened, informal version as "あり[ari]."

EXAMPLES
「ここからの逆転（ぎゃくてん）は十分あり得る」
It is definitely possible to come back from here
「今日はコーヒーもありだなって思って」
Thought it could also be a good idea to have coffee today
