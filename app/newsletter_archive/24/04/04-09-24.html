BREAKING NEWS

Tami Takada
The non-profit National Children’s Food Support Center Musubie (全国こども食堂支援センター・むすびえ) runs kitchens, known as 子ども食堂, that provide food for minimal to no cost for children and struggling members of communities around Japan. In Kanagawa, the municipal government called on frozen food companies to help supply the kitchens with meat, which for a long time was too expensive for the centers to afford.

Sara Goto
[o̞jägät͡ɕä]—meaning “parent gacha”, referring to how children cannot choose their parents and is based on luck. A common use of this is in the saying 親ガチャ外れ, or “losing the parent gacha”, referring to having shitty parents.

EXAMPLES
「店員さんに怒鳴る人の子どもって親ガチャ外れでかわいそう」
I feel so bad for the kids with parents that yell at employees
「片親だけど親ガチャ大当たりだったよ!」
My single dad was an incredible parent nonetheless!

LOOK OUT

Bora Shin
"徐に" is often misused as "abruptly; suddenly; unexpectedly (不意に、突然に)," yet its actual meaning is "to take something easy, stay calm, and act or proceed slowly (落ち着いて、ゆっくりと行動する様)." This meaning comes from the character 「徐」, which conveys "gradually; slowly; deliberately; gently." Similarly, there are phrases with similar meanings such as 「徐行（じょこう）」[jyokou] and「徐々（じょじょ）に」[jyojyoni].

EXAMPLES
「言い分を黙（だま）って聞いていた彼は、おもむろに口を開いた」
He had been silently listening to the excuse when he slowly opened his mouth to speak
「道に迷（まよ）ったメアリーはカバンの中から、おもむろにサンドイッチを取り出すと食べ始めた」
Mary, who lost her way, calmly took out a sandwich from her bag and began to eat it
