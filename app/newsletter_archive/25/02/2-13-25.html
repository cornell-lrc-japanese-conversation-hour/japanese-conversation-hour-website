日本の新年、お正月 (Japanese New Year, Shougatsu)
Bora Shin

We hope everyone’s semester has had a nice start!😊😊

It has been a while since the New Year’s Day, but we would like to introduce Japanese New Year’s, お正月(しょうがつ, Shōgatsu), for 2025’s first newsletter.

Taking place on January 1st, お正月 is one of the most important and widely celebrated holidays in Japan. Businesses and schools are closed for a few days for お正月, as people spend time with their family and decorate their homes with symbolic items.

飾り熊手(かざりくまで, kazari-kumade) is one such item which brings about good fortune and prosperity. There is a tradition of enlarging its size little by little each year, hoping for greater success and happiness than the previous year. It is traditionally ideal to display 飾り熊手 on an altar or high shelf and facing the entrance where it can bring in wealth and good luck from outside.

With traditions such as 御節料理 (おせちりょうり, osechi-ryōri) and relaxing at home, people sometimes worry about 正月太り (しょうがつぶとり, Shōgatsu Butori), which is gaining weight over the New Year’s holiday.

It is also a convention to clean one’s home (大掃除, おおそうじ, ō-souji) on お正月, as the act of cleaning is believed to purify the space and welcome the new year with a fresh start.
