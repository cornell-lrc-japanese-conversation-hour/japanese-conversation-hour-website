早口言葉に挑戦！
Cougar Hashikura

早口言葉（はやぐちことば）is the Japanese word for a tongue-twister, a form of wordplay which works essentially the same way as it does in English. Japanese tongue-twisters use tricks like repeating sounds, syllables that are difficult to pronounce consecutively, and sounds with complicated movements of the tongue to create vocal challenges that often take many tries to say correctly even once. In this newsletter, I’ll list out some of my favorite ones; I encourage you to try your hand–or tongue–at them!


すもももももももものうち（李も桃も桃のうち）

In the genre of repeating sounds, the phrase 「すもももももももものうち」is a mouthful of a sentence that uses 「も」eight times in a row! It can be broken down into the following parts: 「すもも」(Japanese plums) 「も」(...too) 「もも」(Peaches) 「も」(...too) 「もも」(Peaches, this time as a category of fruit) 「のうち」(In the category of) The tongue-twister can roughly be translated as “Japanese plums and peaches are both within the category of peaches.”


うらにわにはにわ、にわにはにわにわとりがいます（裏庭には二羽、庭には二羽鶏がいます）

This is a similar beast of a tongue-twister that uses the couplet 「にわ」(or the equivalent sounding 「には」) seven times in a row! This one can be broken down into the following parts: 「うらにわ」(Backyard) 「には」(At…there are) 「にわ、」(Two birds) 「にわ」(Front yard) 「には」(At…there are) 「にわ」(Two birds) 「にわとり」(Chicken) 「がいます」(Are there) The phrase in full can be translated as “There are two chickens in the backyard, and two chickens in the front yard.”


となりのきゃくはよくかきくうきゃくだ（隣の客はよく柿食う客だ）

This last one falls into the category of ones that are tricky because of the many similar, but separate sounds said in rapid succession, like 「かき」,「きゃく」, and 「くう」. In English, it roughly means “The customer besides me is a customer that eats persimmons a lot.”

Don’t worry if you “bite” on the words, as it might be said in Japanese! When it comes to lines and words, the term “to fumble one’s speech” is typically written as 「噛む」（かむ）which is also how to write “to bite,” or “to chew.” Messing up tongue-twisters is seen as “chewing on the words,” so take a second to chew through the phrases before trying again!
