Graduation Season in Japan 🎓🌸✨
Bora Shin

Commemorating just one month left for our fellow seniors, the theme of this week’s newsletter is 卒業 (そつぎょう; graduation)!

As the school year wraps up in Japan, 卒業式 (そつじょうしき; graduation ceremony) takes the center stage. Unlike in the U.S. where graduation often happens in May or June, Japanese graduation ceremonies are usually held in March.

🎓 A Formal ceremony

Typically held in the school gym, graduation ceremonies in Japan are very structured and solemn. Students gather, sing the national anthem and the school song, and walk up one by one to receive their diplomas–a scene not too unfamiliar to many of us. For attires, middle school and high school students usually wear their school uniforms, while college students, especially women, often wear 袴 (はかま; hakama).

🌸 桜 (さくら; Cherry Blossoms)

In Japan, graduation season aligns with spring and the blooming of 桜. The image of cherry blossoms falling gently during or after the ceremony is iconic with its fleeting beauty.

🎤 Songs and Farewells

Some classic graduation songs include “旅立ちの日に” (たびだちのひに; On the Day of Departure) and “3月9日” (さんがつここのか; March 9th). These songs are often performed by choirs or played during slideshows, making the moment more emotional. After the ceremony, students might exchange letters, messages in yearbooks, or even buttons from their uniforms (especially the second button, which is traditionally given to someone you like💌).

As spring seems to have finally arrived in Ithaca, we wish you all a happy last week of April, and hope you all have a time to enjoy the sun! We hope this season brings you reflection, excitement, and inspiration!✨
