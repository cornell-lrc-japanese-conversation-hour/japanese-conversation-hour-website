四字熟語, Seasons, and Spring🌼🌸🌹
Bora Shin

Although it is still quite chilly in Ithaca, spring is (hopefully) not so far away!

Today, we would like to introduce several 四字熟語 (よじじゅくご; yoji-jyukugo) to wish you all a warm and joyful spring break.

四字熟語 are four-character compounds or idiomatic phrases made up of kanji. Learning 四字熟語 is a fantastic way to deepen your understanding of kanji’s meanings and pronunciation while enriching your Japanese vocabulary!

Before diving into spring-themed 四字熟語, let’s introduce one that represents all four seasons:

🌸 春夏秋冬 (しゅんかしゅうとう; shunkashuutoo)

This 四字熟語 literally means “spring, summer, autumn, and winter.” Notice how kanji are pronounced differently when read in 音読み (おんよみ; on-yomi), Chinese reading of the character, and 訓読み (くんよみ; kun-yomi), Japanese reading of the character.

音読み: しゅん・か・しゅう・とう

訓読み: はる・なつ・あき・ふゆ

In Japan, the concept of 四季 (しき; shiki), four seasons, plays a significant role in daily life, arts, and literature.

Spring-themed 四字熟語

🌿 小春日和 (こはるびより; koharu-biyori)
Literal meaning: Little spring weather
Idiomatic meaning: This phrase is often used to describe unexpectedly mild weather during colder seasons.

🌺 落花流水 (らっかりゅうすい; rakka-ryuusui)
Literal meaning: Falling flowers and flowing water
Idiomatic meaning: This phrase symbolizes the fleeting beauty of and admiration for nature.

🍂 春花秋月 (しゅんかしゅうげつ; shunka-shuugetsu)
Literal meaning: Spring flowers and autumn moon
Idiomatic meaning: This phrase is a poetic way to describe beautiful seasonal scenery, appreciating the beauty and transience of nature’s beauty.

We hope these 四字熟語 bring you a bit of warmth and beauty as we await the arrival of spring! ✨
