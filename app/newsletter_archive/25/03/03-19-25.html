Masterpieces of the Edo Period
Cougar Hashikura

Perhaps the most prominent and well-known style of historical Japanese art is the 浮世絵 (うきよえ, Ukiyo-e) style, which was thoroughly explored between the 17th and 19th centuries. The term 浮世絵 is quite literal; it can be read of as the images (絵) of the “floating,” or fleeting world (浮世). To reflect that, 浮世絵 art utilizes a dream-like, flowing artstyle to depict the transient beauties of life in colorful, expressive ways.

One of the most significant relics of the 浮世絵 period is the 富嶽三十六景 (ふがくさんじゅうろっけい) or the 36 views of Mount Fuji. Produced between 1830 to 1832 by the 浮世絵 master 葛飾北斎 (かつしかほくさい), the series is composed of (ironically enough) 46 浮世絵 paintings, each depicting a different view of Japan’s tallest mountain, 富士山 (ふじさん). Some of the paintings depict the mountain hidden beneath a peaceful sea of clouds, while others show groups of people pausing to observe the mountain from afar.

The first painting in the series has grown to become one of Japan’s most well-known and well-lauded paintings, which you may have seen at the Metropolitan Museum of Art! Titled as the 神奈川沖浪裏 (かながわおきなみうら) or, as it is known in English, The Great Wave off Kanagawa, the painting shows a tumultuous, tsunami-like wave jostling three boats, with the view of 富士山 peeking through from underneath the curling figure of the wave. The 神奈川沖浪裏 is a pinnacle piece for the 浮世絵 era, using bold color contrasts and a high level of detail to depict a moment within the natural world that, like all other beautiful things, only lasts for a moment.

The 浮世絵 movement would eventually go on to motivate the cultural and artistic perception of Japan from European nations, pushing the Japonisme movement in France and inspiring other significant artists like Claude Monet, and Vincent Van Gogh. A great example of the influence 浮世絵 had on art in other nations is “La Japonaise”, a painting made by Monet in 1876, depicting a European woman donning a Japanese kimono, surrounded by うちわ (fans) each depicting a landscape in the 浮世絵 style.
