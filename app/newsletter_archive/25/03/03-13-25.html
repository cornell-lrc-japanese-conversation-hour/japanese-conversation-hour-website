History in Action: NHK Taiga Dramas
Tami Takada

Have you ever been interested in learning more about Japanese history? Every year, NHK broadcasts a year-long production depicting the life of one historical figure and the era they lived in, known as a 大河ドラマ (Taiga Drama). Episodes are broadcasted live every Sunday night, and last around forty-five minutes each. They typically feature huge casts including many star actors and actresses and are known for high quality production and historical accuracy.

This year’s Taiga, 『べらぼう〜蔦重栄華乃夢噺〜』(Berabo ~The Story of Tsutaju’s Rise~), follows the story of Tsutaya Juzaburo (蔦屋重三郎) [b. 1750, d. 1797], an Edo-era publisher that helped print works from world-famous ukiyoe (浮世絵) artists like Kitagawa Utamaro (喜多川歌麿) and Katsushika Hokusai (葛飾北斎). 蔦重 (Tsutaya’s nickname) is played by idol Yokohama Ryusei (横浜流星), known for roles in the 2022 movie Wandering (流浪の月) and a plethora of TV shows. Other notable appearances include narrator Ayase Haruka (綾瀬はるか) and Tanuma Okitsugu (田沼意次) played by Watanabe Ken (渡辺謙).

Although Taiga lacks popularity amongst younger audiences, it is known for its large impact on domestic tourism and shaping the popular image of history. One of the biggest Taigas made, 2010’s 『龍馬伝』(Ryomaden) following Bakumatsu samurai Sakamoto Ryoma, supposedly brought about 5.35 billion yen of revenue in domestic tourism.

My personal recommendation is 2022’s 『鎌倉殿の十三人』(The 13 Lords of the Shogun), which charts the rise and fall of the Minamoto dynasty and future puppet master Hojo clan. The story is set at the start of the Kamakura era, the first real era of bushi (samurai) that comes off the Heian era, which is known instead for its high court culture.
