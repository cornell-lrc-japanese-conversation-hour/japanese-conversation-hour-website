敬語: Japanese Honorific Speech
Bora Shin

敬語 (けいご; Keigo) is a system of respectful language in Japanese, used to show politeness and social hierarchy. 敬語 is essential in Japanese communication, especially in formal settings such as business, customer service, and interactions with superiors or elders.

There are three major categories of 敬語:

  1. 尊敬語 (そんけいご; sonkeigo)
    尊敬語 elevates the listener by using special verb forms. This is used when speaking about someone of higher status.
    [EX] 行く (いく; iku; to go) → いらっしゃる (irassharu)
    [例文] いらっしゃいませ！(Welcome!; A common polite greeting used in stores, restaurants, etc; the subject of the verb “いらっしゃる” is the customer here: お客様がいらっしゃいました)
  2. 謙譲語 (けんじょうご; kenjougo)
    謙譲語, in contrasts with 尊敬語, humbles the speaker or the speaker’s in-group in order to show deference to others. This form is often used when referring to one’s own actions in formal settings.
    [EX 1] 行く (いく; iku; to go) → 参る (まいる; mairu)
    [EX 2]言う (いう; iu; to tell/say) → 申す (もうす; mousu)
    [例文]
    先生 (teacher): レポートについて何か質問がありますか？ (Do you have any questions about the report?)
    私 (me!): はい、一点申し上げたいことがございます。(Yes, there is one thing I would like to humbly mention.)
  3. 丁寧語 (ていねいご; teineigo)
    丁寧語 is a general polite language used in everyday conversations. It primarily involves “~です,” “~ます,” and “～ございます” at the end of the sentence to make speech more polite.
    [EX] 行く (いく; iku; to go) → 行きます (いきます; ikimasu)
    [例文]
    上司 (boss/manager): 会議室はどこですか？ (Where is the conference room?)
    私 (me!): 3階にございます。(It’s on the third floor.)
